Practical Safeguarding in Ministry

Serving Safely with Children and Vulnerable Adults

Safeguarding is not only about responding to abuse when it occurs. It is also about creating ministry environments that reduce the risk of harm and promote safety for everyone.

The following guidelines help ensure that ministry with children and vulnerable adults is conducted in ways that are safe, transparent, and accountable.

Part A

Practical Safeguarding with Children

  • Always have at least two approved volunteers present — never be alone with a child.
  • If alone unexpectedly: keep the door open, stay in a visible space, move to a public area where possible.
  • Ministry spaces should have see-through windows or open doors during all sessions.
  • Appropriate physical contact: high fives, handshakes, fist bumps, brief side hugs initiated by the child.
  • Avoid: sitting children on your lap, tickling, piggyback rides, holding children unnecessarily, or assisting in the restroom alone.
  • Never message children privately, add them on social media, or share personal contact details.
  • All communication through official ministry channels or group chats with other leaders present.
  • If a child is injured: inform the ministry leader, complete an incident report, and notify parents — never ignore an incident.

Part B

Safeguarding Vulnerable Adults

  • Empowerment — support individuals to make their own decisions; respect their choices and dignity.
  • Prevention — maintain clear ministry boundaries and avoid situations where unhealthy dependency may develop.
  • Proportionality — respond appropriately to the level of risk. When in doubt, seek guidance from a Safeguarding Officer.
  • Protection — give extra care to those with limited support networks or who struggle to advocate for themselves.
  • Partnership — safeguarding is never done alone. Work with ministry leaders, the safeguarding team, and appropriate professionals.
  • Avoid dependency relationships — pastoral care should never rely on a single individual.
  • Never provide personal financial support, give significant gifts, or offer regular one-to-one counselling without oversight.
  • If someone raises safeguarding concerns: do not handle it alone — inform a Safeguarding Officer, ministry leader, or HTBB staff member immediately.
